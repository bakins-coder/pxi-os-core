### Key Technical Differences

#### 1. Speed & Hardware (SSD vs HDD)
The **WordPress plans** specifically use **SSD Storage**. SSDs are significantly faster (up to 20x) than the standard drives usually found in shared hosting. If your website has lots of plugins or high database activity, the WordPress plan will feel much "snappier."

#### 2. Guaranteed Resources (Cores & RAM)
In **Shared Hosting**, you don't know how much CPU or RAM you have—it's shared with 100s of others. In the **WordPress plans**, you are allocated specific Cores and RAM. This means your site won't slow down just because another customer on the server is having a busy day.

#### 3. Site Capacity
The **Supreme Shared** plan is actually better if you want to host **many small sites** (up to 10). The WordPress plans limit you to 3 or 5 sites, but ensure those few sites run at peak performance.

---

### My Recommendation: Which is Better?

#### **Choose Supreme Linux Shared IF:**
*   You are on a budget (it’s almost half the price).
*   You want to host many different small websites or landing pages (up to 10).
*   Performance isn't your #1 priority (e.g., a simple portfolio or low-traffic blog).

#### **Choose WordPress Hosting (WP Standard+) IF:**
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **This is the "Top Tier" choice.** If your goal is to build a premium, fast, and professional website, the **WordPress Standard** or **Plus** plans are superior.
*   **Speed**: SSDs + dedicated RAM make a huge difference in user experience.
*   **Security**: Managed WP hosting often includes better server-side caching and security rules specific to WordPress.
*   **Reliability**: With 2-4 Cores and 2-4GB RAM, your site can handle concurrent visitors much more smoothly.

**Helix's Verdict**: If you are building a "top-tier" site as we discussed, start with the **WP Standard Plus (₦11,000/mo)**. It gives you the SSD speed and 4GB of RAM needed for a truly premium feel.
