# Roadmap: From Shared Hosting to the Cloud

**Prepared by: Helix (Full-Stack Web Architect)**
**For: Akin**

You’ve identified a key conflict: you want the **flexibility** of hosting multiple sites (Shared) but want the **premium performance** of WordPress hosting, and eventual **cloud scalability**. Here is the roadmap I recommend.

---

### Phase 1: The Multi-Site Launchpad (Shared Hosting)
If you are planning to launch several different websites at once, the **Supreme Linux Shared** plan is your best starting point.

*   **Why**: You can use those **10 Addon Domains** to experiment with different ideas without paying for 10 separate hosting accounts.
*   **The Strategy**: Build your sites here first. When one specific site starts getting traction (regular visitors, business value), that is the "Signal" to move.

### Phase 2: The Vertical Scale (Managed WordPress)
Once a specific site is performing well, you move it to its own **Managed WordPress Plan**.

*   **When to Move**: 
    1.  Your site feels sluggish despite optimizations.
    2.  You see "Resource Limit Reached" errors in cPanel.
    3.  The site is now your primary source of income or business.
*   **Is it hard to move?**: No. Within the same provider, they often do it for free. If moving providers, we can use a "Migration Plugin" to clone the site and move it in about 30 minutes.

### Phase 3: The Cloud Endgame (AWS / Google Cloud / Vercel)
"Cloud Hosting" isn't just a server; it's a cluster of resources that can grow and shrink automatically.

*   **When to Move**:
    1.  You have massive, unpredictable traffic spikes (e.g., viral marketing).
    2.  You need 100% uptime (Cloud infrastructure has no single point of failure).
    3.  You are building a complex Web App (like the PXI OS Core we are working on) rather than a simple content site.
*   **The Benefit**: You only pay for what you use. If nobody visits at 3 AM, you pay pennies. If 10,000 people visit at noon, the cloud adds more power automatically to keep it fast.
